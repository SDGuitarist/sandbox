## Research Summary

- 
Envato's *Beyond Adoption: The State of AI in Creative Work 2026* (1,780 global creative professionals surveyed): nearly half (49%) use AI daily for client work, but nearly 3 in 4 (69%) don't feel fully prepared for an AI-driven industry. The report calls this "a critical gap between adoption and mastery."


- 
The real pressure on experienced creatives isn't resistance to AI. It's business models. 29% of Gen X creatives cite "clients assuming AI makes everything faster and cheaper" as a significant concern, compared to just 18% of Gen Z. Western creatives face six times more pressure to prove human creative value alongside AI than counterparts in Asia and the Middle East.


- 
58% of creative professionals have used AI in client work without disclosing it. Only 31% of creatives always disclose when AI is involved.
 The industry is operating without a shared framework for what "human-led" even means.

- 
Research on peer learning among adult learners shows that adults who engage in structured peer-based discussions and activities tend to retain information better than those who rely solely on traditional instructional methods. Peer discussions also allow learners to distribute cognitive effort, making complex information more digestible and increasing the likelihood of retention.


- 
A meta-analysis by Pai et al. (2015) across 24 studies found an overall effect size of 0.30 in favor of small group learning over individual learning on transfer tasks
 — meaning not just better retention, but better real-world application of what was learned.
