## Research Summary

- 
**BCG "Where's the Value in AI?" (Oct. 2024, 1,000 CxOs across 59 countries):** 74% of companies have yet to show tangible value from their AI investments. The companies that ARE getting results achieved 1.5x higher revenue growth and 1.6x greater shareholder returns over three years.


- 
**The BCG "70/20/10" rule:** AI leaders follow a formula of putting 70% of resources into people and processes, 20% into technology and data, and only 10% into algorithms. Most companies mistakenly do the opposite, prioritizing technical challenges over human factors.


- 
**Harvard Business Publishing/Degreed Study:** AI-fluent professionals (defined as frequent users of gen AI who also have strong understanding of its capabilities) were far more likely to report stronger outcomes. Among them, 81% said they were more productive, 54% were more creative, and 53% were better prepared to solve complex business challenges.


- 
**IE University research on the "Danger Zone":** When AI fabricates plausible-sounding analysis in domains a person doesn't understand, the failure goes undetected. The core problem: "You can't develop judgment about what you don't know." The productivity gains cited in popular AI studies are drawn from highly skilled professionals who developed their expertise *before* AI existed.


- 
**Microsoft 2025 Future of Work Report:** Real effectiveness requires three types of expertise working together: domain expertise, expertise in working with AI, and expertise in managing AI systems. The research finds that effectiveness comes from the *interaction* between them, not from any one in isolation. Experienced professionals selectively delegate routine tasks to AI while deliberately staying close to higher-order work like interpretation, synthesis, and decision-making.
