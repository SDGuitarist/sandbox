## Research Summary

- 
A 2025 Thryv national survey found small business AI adoption jumped from 39% in 2024 to 55% in 2025, a 41% increase. Content marketing emerged as the most popular use case, with 80% of small business AI users saying it's essential to reaching new customers.


- 
About two-thirds of small business AI users are using the tools for marketing tasks: writing social media posts, generating ad copy, and designing graphics. Business owners cite consistency and visibility as the main draws.


- 
Among musicians specifically, 60% are utilizing AI tools in their projects, and AI has become part of the creative workflow for 36.8% of music producers.


- 
The U.S. Chamber of Commerce found that 58% of small businesses currently use generative AI, up from 40% in 2024. Critically, 82% of the smallest businesses (under 5 employees) cite believing AI isn't applicable to their business as the primary reason for non-adoption — suggesting an education gap, not an applicability gap.


- 
Two-thirds of small business AI users say it takes pressure off themselves and their staff, and 41% believe it will help them navigate economic uncertainty, with optimism highest among service-based businesses.
