# Template Effectiveness

Which outreach templates convert, which don't, and why. Based on April 20, 2026 batch (30 sends).

## What Makes the Top Two Work

### Connectors Template (33% warm)

> I'm running a workshop April 25 on using AI for small business. Not the hype version. Practical systems for leads, follow-ups, client communication. Half day, 30 seats, hands-on.
>
> I'm not sure if it's your thing, but I bet you know 2-3 people who'd get a lot out of it. Happy to send details if you want to pass it along.

**Why it converts:**
- Doesn't ask them to attend. Asks them to refer. Removes the purchase decision entirely.
- "I'm not sure if it's your thing" = anti-pitch. Disarms sales resistance.
- "I bet you know 2-3 people" = flattery as CTA. Positions them as a gatekeeper with influence.
- Self-selection mechanism: Madison Keith asked for details for herself, even though the template didn't ask her to attend. The indirect ask let her opt in without being sold to.

**When to use:** People with large networks, media presence, community roles. People who introduce others.

**When NOT to use:** People with followers but no connector identity (artists, fitness influencers, nonprofit EDs who don't see themselves as networkers).

### Writers Template (20% warm)

> I've been using AI to run my business, not for writing but for everything around the writing. Leads, client communication, follow-ups, the operational stuff that eats creative time.
>
> I'm teaching a workshop April 25 on how to build AI systems around your expertise. Not generic AI tips. Your voice, your standards, your workflow.
>
> Think you'd be into it?

**Why it converts:**
- "Not for writing, but for everything around the writing" = best disarming line in the batch. Neutralizes the #1 writer objection (AI replacing my craft) in one sentence.
- "Your voice, your standards, your workflow" = reassurance that they keep control.
- "Think you'd be into it?" = casual, non-pressuring close.

**When to use:** Any craft-identity segment where the person might fear AI replaces their skill (writers, designers, musicians, photographers).

**When NOT to use:** Segments where efficiency is the explicit value prop (operations, sales, admin-heavy roles).

## What Makes the Bottom Two Fail

### Real Estate Template (0/9 warm)

> Quick question. How fast do you respond when a new lead comes in? I used to be inconsistent about that and it was costing me business.
>
> I built AI systems that handle my lead responses and follow-ups. Every inquiry gets my best response, with my pricing logic, in minutes instead of days. I'm teaching how to build this on April 25. Small workshop, hands-on.
>
> Worth a look?

**Why it fails:**
- "How fast do you respond when a new lead comes in?" = a question every CRM vendor, coaching program, and lead gen tool has asked them. Triggers instant pattern matching: "this is a pitch."
- "I used to be inconsistent" = confession that creates distance. Agents who are closing deals feel their system works. They don't identify with "inconsistent."
- The template positions the recipient as having a problem. Agents closing $500K+ deals don't want to be told their lead response is slow by a musician they know from Facebook.

**Recommended rewrite direction:**
- Drop the problem-framing entirely.
- Lead with what you built and what it freed up: "I built AI systems that handle the 40 hours a week that aren't selling. Scheduling, follow-ups, listing descriptions, market reports. Freed me up to do the actual work."
- Or reframe as connector ask: RE agents might be better referral sources than attendees.

### Small Biz Template (0/6 warm, but got best competitive intel)

> Quick one. I've been using AI to run my business development for the past year and it's changed everything. Not ChatGPT tips. Actual systems that handle leads, follow-ups, and client communication with my voice and my standards.
>
> I'm running a small workshop April 25 teaching how to build this for your own business. 30 seats, hands-on, laptops open.
>
> Is this something you'd be interested in?

**Why it underperforms:**
- "Business development" framing appeals to sales-oriented operators. Craft-oriented operators (gallery owner, caterer, community college ED) don't think of their work as "business development."
- Credibility assertion ("changed everything") without proof. Compare to writers template which shows understanding of their specific pain ("the operational stuff that eats creative time").

**Recommended rewrite direction:**
- Swap "business development" for craft-specific framing: "the 20 hours a week that aren't the work you love."
- Add the fear-disarming pattern from writers template: "Not replacing what you do. Handling what gets in the way of what you do."

## CTA Patterns

| CTA | Template | Effect | Result |
|-----|----------|--------|--------|
| "I bet you know 2-3 people" | Connectors | Role-giving, indirect | Best performer |
| "Think you'd be into it?" | Writers | Casual, non-pressuring | Second best |
| "Worth a look?" | Real Estate | Soft but still direct | No conversions |
| "Is this something you'd be interested in?" | Small Biz | Yes/no binary | No conversions |
| "Sound useful?" | Wellness | Soft but still direct | No conversions (n=2) |
| "Want me to send you the details?" | Musicians | Offer, not ask | No conversions (n=1) |

**Pattern:** CTAs that give the recipient a role ("you know people") or lower the stakes ("think you'd be into it?") outperform CTAs that ask for a decision ("is this something you'd be interested in?"). The more binary the CTA, the easier it is to ignore.

---

## Template Design Rules (for future batches)

1. **Disarm the segment-specific fear first.** Writers fear replacement. Wellness practitioners fear losing the personal touch. RE agents fear being pitched. Name the fear and neutralize it before pitching.

2. **Frame AI as freeing up their identity work, not optimizing their funnel.** "So you can be present with clients" (wellness) > "handles your lead response" (real estate). Craft-first framing > efficiency-first framing for identity-driven operators.

3. **Indirect CTA > direct CTA.** Give them a role or lower the stakes. Don't force a yes/no decision in the first message.

4. **One template per segment is not enough.** Real estate agents and wellness coaches are both "service providers" but need completely different framing. Segment by identity, not by industry category.

5. **The template is the conversion lever. The opener is the read lever.** A great opener gets them to read the message. A great template gets them to reply. Both matter independently. Don't invest all effort in personalization and default on the template.
