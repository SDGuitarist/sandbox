## LinkedIn Post

Most people are trying to get faster answers out of AI. The more useful skill is knowing when not to trust the fast answer.

Here is the check I teach beginners. Three questions, about ten seconds, before you act on anything a model hands you.

1. Source. Where did it get this? A model can produce a confident paragraph with nothing real underneath it. If it cannot point to something you could verify, treat the claim as a starting guess, not a fact.

2. Stake. What breaks if this is wrong? Not every answer needs the same scrutiny. A first draft of a caption is low stake. A number in a client proposal, a legal date, a medical claim, those are high stake. Match your checking to what is on the line.

3. Sense. Does it line up with what you already know from doing the work? You have years of pattern recognition in your field. When an answer feels off, that instinct is information. Slow down and look closer.

None of this is about distrusting the tool. It is about staying in the loop. That is what Human-Led AI means to me. The model does the fast part. You keep the judgment, because the judgment is the part that carries your name.

The people getting real value from AI right now are not the ones who trust it the most. They are the ones who know exactly when to check it.

Try the three questions on your next AI answer and notice how often at least one of them changes what you do.
