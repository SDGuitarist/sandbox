## LinkedIn Post

Most people trying to get better at AI are trying to prompt faster. The better move is to prompt deeper.

Here's the structure I teach beginners. Five layers. It works because it front-loads the context a model needs before it answers, instead of making it guess.

1. Identity. Tell the AI who it is. "You are a booking agent for a jazz trio." Role sets tone, vocabulary, and priorities in one line.

2. World. Give it the situation. The venue, the budget, the history, the stakes. Context it can't infer, you have to supply.

3. Task. Say what you actually want out. Not "help me with this" but "write the follow-up email."

4. Example. Show it one sample of what good looks like. A model matches patterns. Give it a pattern to match.

5. Constraints. Set the limits. Length, tone, what to avoid. This is where you get your own voice back instead of generic AI filler.

I've watched people who had never written a prompt use this and get a usable result on the first try. Not because they got faster. Because they went one layer deeper than "write me a post about X."

The tool isn't the skill. The structure of what you ask is the skill.

Try the five layers on your next prompt and notice what changes.
