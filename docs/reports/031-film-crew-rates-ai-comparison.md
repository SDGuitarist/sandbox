# Film Crew Rates: Industry Standard Pricing (2025-2026)

Research compiled April 30, 2026. Raw rate data for roles most likely to be replaced or augmented by AI.

---

## 1. Composer / Music Scoring

### Per-Minute of Finished Music
| Tier | Rate |
|------|------|
| Student/emerging | $50-$100/min |
| Professional indie | $200-$500/min |
| Established professional | $500-$1,000/min |
| Top-tier / studio | $1,000+/min |

### Per-Project
| Project Type | Low End | Mid Range | High End |
|-------------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Short film (10-15 min of music) | $1,500-$5,000 | $5,000-$10,000 | $10,000-$25,000 |
| Indie feature (30-50 min of music) | $2,000-$10,000 | $10,000-$25,000 | $25,000-$100,000 |
| Studio feature | $50,000-$100,000 | $100,000-$500,000 | $500,000-$2,000,000+ |

### Budget Percentage
- Standard guideline: 5%-10% of overall production budget (includes recording costs, not just composer fee)
- Indie alternative: Some composers charge ~$700/min if music can be reused as library music, ~$1,200/min for exclusive rights

### Union Status
- No dedicated composers' union in film (unlike theater). AFM (American Federation of Musicians) covers session musicians for recording.
- AFM scale for session players adds significant cost when using live orchestra.

### Library Music Alternative
- Royalty-free/stock music: $15-$50 per track (one-time license)
- Premium library music: $100-$500 per track
- Custom library music services: $500-$2,000 for a package

## 2. VFX Artist

### Per-Shot Costs
| Shot Complexity | Approx. Cost Per Shot |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Wire removal, cleanup, paint-out | $100-$500 |
| Sky replacement, simple compositing | $500-$2,000 |
| Green screen keying + environment | $1,000-$5,000 |
| Set extension, matte painting | $2,000-$10,000 |
| CG creature/character (hero shot) | $50,000-$150,000+ |
| Complex destruction/simulation | $100,000+ |

### Freelance Day/Hourly Rates
| Tier | Hourly | Daily (8hr) |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Junior freelance | $19-$25/hr | $150-$200/day |
| Mid-level freelance | $35-$50/hr | $280-$400/day |
| Senior freelance | $50-$75/hr | $400-$600/day |
| Lead/Supervisor | $75-$125/hr | $600-$1,000/day |

### Per-Minute Rates (Full VFX sequences)
- Basic CGI: $1,000-$1,500/min
- High-quality 3D VFX: $2,000-$5,000/min
- Complex CGI-heavy sequences: $50,000+/min

### Marketplace Rates (Fiverr/Upwork, for reference)
- Wire/object removal: $15-$50/shot
- Rotoscoping and green screen: $30-$100/shot
- Compositing and tracking: $100-$500/shot

### Union Status
- IATSE covers VFX artists at union facilities. Many VFX houses are non-union.
- AI is already reducing costs 20-40% on rotoscoping, cleanup, wire removal, and crowd generation.
- Hero creative work (CG creatures, digital doubles, complex simulations) still requires senior artist supervision.

### Budget Benchmarks
- 100-shot invisible effects sequence (cleanup, sky replacement): ~$500K total (~$5,000/shot avg)
- 40-shot hero CG creature sequence: $6M+ ($150,000/shot avg)
- Mid-tier streaming: 80-250 shots/episode, $600K-$1.8M/episode VFX budget
- Revision impact: 4 rounds of creative notes can cost 2-3x the original bid

## 3. Storyboard Artist

### Per-Frame Rates
| Tier | Per Frame |
|------|-----------|
| Entry-level / student | $10-$25/frame |
| Mid-level professional | $25-$50/frame |
| Senior / studio-level | $50-$100/frame |
| Cinematic / highly detailed | $100-$200+/frame |

### Day Rates
| Tier | Daily | Weekly |
|------|-------|--------|
| Mid-level | $150-$350/day | $1,000-$1,500/week |
| High-level | $450-$700/day | $2,000-$3,000/week |

### Hourly Rates
| Tier | Hourly |
|------|--------|
| Mid-level | $30-$50/hr |
| High-level | $60-$85/hr |

### Union Status
- IATSE Local 839 (Animation Guild) covers storyboard artists in animation.
- Union storyboard artists saw ~4% increase in unit rates, plus 28% increase for theatrical subjects 75+ minutes.
- Storyboard Pilot Unit rates were added to the Animation Guild wage table effective March 23, 2025.
- Live-action film storyboards are typically non-union freelance work.

### Project Estimates
- Short film (50-100 frames): $500-$5,000
- Feature film (500-1,500 frames): $5,000-$50,000+
- Rush orders: 1.5x-2x standard rate

## 4. Screenwriter / Script Doctor

### WGA Minimums (Effective May 2, 2025 -- 3.5% increase over prior year)

#### High Budget (film budget $5M+)
| Work Type | Minimum |
|-----------|---------|
| Original screenplay including treatment | $147,920 |
| Screenplay excluding treatment | $102,288 |
| First draft screenplay | $68,205 |
| Final draft / rewrite | $45,470 |
| Treatment | $45,470 |
| Polish | $22,736 |

#### Low Budget (film budget under $5M)
| Work Type | Minimum |
|-----------|---------|
| Screenplay (original, including treatment) | ~$77,495 |
| Other categories | Proportionally lower |

#### WGA Low Budget Agreement (budget at or below $1.2M)
- Films $200K-$499K: Writer and company may elect to pay 50% of Schedule of Minimums
- Films $500K-$1.2M: Full WGA minimums apply but with fewer requirements

### Non-WGA / Indie Rates
| Tier | Range |
|------|-------|
| Student/micro-budget | $500-$5,000 (flat fee or deferred) |
| Low-budget indie ($50K-$500K budget) | $2,500-$15,000 |
| Professional indie ($500K-$2M budget) | $10,000-$40,000 |
| Script doctor / punch-up (non-WGA) | $1,500-$10,000/week |

### Budget Percentage
- Common indie guideline: ~2.5% of total film budget for screenplay

### Union Status
- WGA MBA runs through May 1, 2026. A new 2026 MBA has been negotiated.
- Rate increases in 2023 MBA: 5% (Sept 2023), 4% (May 2024), 3.5% (May 2025).
- Median WGA member compensation (no prior screen credits): $300,000. Maximum reported: $700,000.

## 5. Voice Actor

### SAG-AFTRA Theatrical Rates (Effective July 1, 2025)
| Agreement | Day Rate | Weekly Rate |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Basic Theatrical | $1,246/day | $4,326/week |
| Low Budget ($700K-$2M) | $810/day | $2,812/week |
| Moderate Low Budget ($300K-$700K) | $436/day | $1,514/week |
| Modified Low Budget | $335/day | $1,166/week |
| Ultra Low Budget / Short Film | $249/day | -- |

### SAG-AFTRA Voice-Specific Rates
| Work Type | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Audio commercial session (solo/duo) | $404.30-$420.50/session |
| Audiobook narration (member minimum) | $210/finished hour |
| Interactive media (video games, up to 3 voices/4 hrs) | $1,134.95/session |
| Interactive media (6-10 voices/6 hrs) | $2,270.78/session |

### Non-Union Voice Actor Rates
| Work Type | Low | Mid | High |
|-----------|-----|-----|------|
| Audiobook narration (PFH) | $150-$250 | $250-$500 | $500-$1,000+ |
| E-learning (per finished minute) | $15-$25 | $25-$40 | $40-$55 |
| Non-broadcast narration (up to 2 min) | $200-$350 | $350-$500 | $500-$750 |
| Character work (animation/games, per session) | $200-$500 | $500-$1,000 | $1,000-$2,500 |

### Pension & Health
- SAG-AFTRA P&H contribution: 21% of covered earnings (performers), 20.5% (background)
- Increases total cost above stated minimums

### Project Rates
| Project Type | Range |
|-------------|-------|
| Short film (under 30 min) | $500-$5,000 |
| Indie feature | $5,000-$25,000 |
| Professional indie feature | $15,000-$50,000 |

### IATSE / Editors Guild (Local 700) Union Rates
- Sound mixing (Schedule A-4): $99.76/hr, weekly guarantee $4,125.65 (effective Aug 3, 2025)
- Supervising engineer: weekly "on call" $3,748.64/week (journeyperson), $3,279.55/week (entry level)

### Union Status
- IATSE Local 700 (Motion Picture Editors Guild) covers editors, assistant editors, sound editors, and post-production staff on union productions.
- Low Budget Theatrical Agreement has separate (lower) rate schedules.

## 7. Sound Design

### Hourly and Day Rates
| Tier | Hourly | Daily |
|------|--------|-------|
| Entry-level | $25-$50/hr | $200-$400/day |
| Professional | $50-$100/hr | $400-$800/day |
| Senior / supervisor | $100-$150/hr | $800-$1,200/day |

### Per Finished Minute (PFM)
| Tier | Rate |
|------|------|
| Indie / emerging | $20-$50/PFM |
| Professional | $50-$100/PFM |
| High-end | $100-$200+/PFM |

### Full Post-Audio Package (Feature Film)
| Component | Indie Low | Indie Mid | Studio |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Dialogue editing | $1,500-$3,000 | $3,000-$8,000 | $10,000-$25,000 |
| Foley (artist + stage) | $3,000-$6,000 | $6,000-$15,000 | $15,000-$50,000 |
| Sound design/effects | $2,000-$5,000 | $5,000-$15,000 | $15,000-$50,000 |
| ADR (stage + recording) | $1,000-$3,000 | $3,000-$8,000 | $8,000-$25,000 |
| Final mix | $3,000-$8,000 | $8,000-$20,000 | $20,000-$75,000 |
| **Total post-audio** | **$10,000-$25,000** | **$25,000-$65,000** | **$70,000-$225,000+** |

### Time Estimates
- Foley: ~1 hour per linear minute of film. 6+ days for a feature drama.
- Complete audio post: ~5-6 hours per linear minute (dialogue edit, foley, SFX, mix)
- A 90-minute indie feature: minimum ~40 hours for basic sound work

### Budget Guidelines
- Post sound: 2-5% of film budget (general guideline)
- More realistic for quality: 10-15% of total budget for audio post
- Post-production overall (edit + VFX + sound + color + music): 20-30% of total budget

### Union Status
- IATSE covers sound editors and mixers on union productions.

## 8. Colorist / Color Grading

### Hourly Rates
| Tier | Hourly |
|------|--------|
| Entry-level / remote freelance | $25-$65/hr |
| Professional | $65-$200/hr |
| Senior / facility colorist | $200-$500/hr |
| Top-tier (LA/NY facility) | $500-$1,000/hr |

### Day Rates
| Tier | Daily |
|------|-------|
| Junior freelance | $300-$500/day |
| Professional freelance | $700-$1,000/day |
| Facility colorist | $1,000-$2,500/day |

### Project Rates
| Project Type | Range |
|-------------|-------|
| Short film (under 30 min) | $500-$2,000 |
| Indie feature (basic pass) | $2,000-$5,000 |
| Indie feature (detailed grade) | $5,000-$15,000 |
| Studio feature | $15,000-$50,000+ |

### Real-World Example
- 87-minute indie feature, slow and detailed grade: $9,500

### Time Benchmarks
- Experienced colorist: 200-300 shots/day
- Basic correction on a 90-min feature: ~3 days (24-30 hours)
- Detailed grade on a feature: 5-10 days
- Minimum for low-budget: ~40 hours

### Market Trend
- Colorist rates have dropped ~30% in many markets over recent years due to software accessibility (DaVinci Resolve is free) and AI-assisted grading tools.
- IATSE scale: ~$65/hr for colorist work, though market rates exceed this.

### Marketplace Rates (Upwork/Fiverr)
- $10-$60/hr for basic color correction
- Significant quality variance at lower price points

### Union Status
- IATSE covers colorists at union post-production facilities.

## Summary: Cost Comparison Reference

For a filmmaker comparing human hire vs. AI on a **$500K indie feature** with 90 minutes runtime:

| Role | Typical Human Cost (Indie) | Notes |
|------|---------------------------|-------|
| Composer (40 min score) | $8,000-$20,000 | Or $200-$500/min |
| VFX (50 invisible shots) | $5,000-$25,000 | Wire removal, sky replace, cleanup |
| Storyboards (500 frames) | $5,000-$15,000 | Or $25-$50/frame |
| Screenplay | $10,000-$25,000 | ~2.5% of budget or WGA low budget |
| Voice (narrator, 2hr finished) | $500-$1,000 | Non-union PFH rate |
| Editor (12-16 weeks) | $15,000-$40,000 | Or $500-$800/day |
| Sound design (full post) | $15,000-$35,000 | Dialogue, foley, SFX, mix |
| Colorist (5-7 days) | $3,500-$10,000 | Or $700-$1,000/day |
| **Total for these roles** | **$62,000-$171,000** | |
